About

I am a second-year M.S. student at the University of Massachusetts Amherst studying computer science. I'm interested in machine learning (particularly deep learning) and computational neuroscience, and how they may be related. I enjoy thinking about computational complexity, statistical inference, information theory, and parallel / distributed programming. I prefer writing code in Python and typically use open source scientific Python libraries for my projects.

I'm a research assistant in the Biologically Inspired Neural and Dynamical Systems Laboratory, where we're using spiking neural networks for machine learning. I did my undergraduate studies at UMass Amherst with a double major in computer science and mathematics.

I come from Dartmouth, which is located in south-eastern Massachusetts, and I currently reside in Amherst. I'm an avid reader / writer / programmer, coffee lover, and proud co-owner of a miniature Australian shepherd named Quinn (pictured above!).

Work Experience

  • Summer 2015: I interned as a software developer with Epsilon Data Management.
  • Fall 2015: I worked with professor David Mix Barrington on an independent study project in circuit complexity theory, in which we tried to characterize the computational power of constant-depth Boolean circuits with composite number counting gates.
  • Spring 2016: I worked with professors David Mix Barrington and Hava T. Siegelmann on an independent study project on the computational complexity of the memcomputing model of computation.
  • Summer 2016: I spent the summer in the BINDS lab, where I used machine learning methods to explore and explain datasets, designed a brain-computer interface experiment, and wrote about computation with dynamical systems.
  • Fall 2016: I was the grader for the cross-listed computer science and mathematics course on combinatorics and graph theory.
  • Fall 2015 - Spring 2017: I was a programmer in the Cognition and Action laboratory, in the department of Cognitive and Brain Sciences at UMass Amherst. I developed software for experiments with human subjects, managed lab equipment, and performend data analysis and processing.
  • Summer 2017: I worked as a research intern in the Air Force Research Lab's Automatic Target Recognition Center. I developed a neural network layer whose weights are updated via a Hebbian learning rule, instead of by SGD + back-propagation.

Current Employment
