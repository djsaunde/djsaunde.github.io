I come from Dartmouth, which is located in south-eastern Massachusetts, and I currently reside in an apartment in the center of the town of Amherst. I recently finished my last season with the UMass drumline, in which I played snare drum.

In the summer of 2015, I interned as a software developer with Epsilon Data Management, where I was able to contribute shipped code in a number of areas, including improved database routines, user interface design, and bug fixes. In the fall of 2015, I worked with professor David Mix Barrington on an independent study project in circuit complexity theory, in which we tried to understand the computational power of constant-depth Boolean circuits with composite number counting gates. In the spring of 2016, I worked on an independent study project jointly with professors David Mix Barrington and Hava T. Siegelmann, in which we investigated the computational complexity of the memcomputing model of computation. I spent the summer iof 2016 working in the BINDS lab, where I used machine learning methods to explore datasets, helping to design an ERP-based brain-computer interface experiment, and read and wrote about computation with dynamical systems.

I am employed as a lab technician / programmer by the COGNAC lab, in the department of Cognitive and Brain Sciences at UMass, where I am responsible for writing and debugging human-participant experiment software, fixing and purchasing lab equipment, doing data analysis and organization, and a bizarre collection of other computer-related tasks. Finally, I am responsible for grading homework assignments (in the fall of 2016) for the computer science / mathematics course in combinatorics and graph theory.
